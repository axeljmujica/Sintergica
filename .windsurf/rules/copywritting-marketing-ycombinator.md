## COPY PHILOSOPHY — APPLY TO EVERY PIECE OF TEXT

### 1. Lead with the problem, not the product
Bad: "Lattice es una plataforma de IA empresarial con agentes autónomos y bases de conocimiento vectoriales."
Good: "Tu equipo legal dedica 15 horas a la semana revisando contratos. Ana, el agente de Lattice, lo hace en 2."

### 2. One insight per headline
Bad: "IA privada, segura, especializada y gobernable para México y LATAM"
Good: "La IA que sabe lo que dice el SAT. No lo que dice el IRS."

### 3. Specificity over generality — always
Bad: "Reducimos tiempos de proceso significativamente"
Good: "−60% en tiempo de revisión contractual. Documentado en clientes del sector legal."

### 4. Write for the skeptic
Every claim needs to be defensible. If you write "the most advanced AI in LATAM," the CFO will ask for proof. Write what you can prove: "Na'at Full — 1T parameters, 32B active — the largest model built in Mexico."

### 5. The body copy answers: "Why should I believe you?"
Features → Benefits → Proof → Call to action. Never skip proof.

### 6. CTA copy = next step, not a command
Bad: "Contáctanos" / "Compra ahora"
Good: "Agenda tu Diagnóstico Inteligente — 45 min, sin costo" / "Ve cómo funciona en tu industria"

### 7. B2G-specific rules
- Lead with compliance and sovereignty, not features
- Reference specific laws: LFPDPPP, LGTAIP, CNBV, COFEPRIS — not generic "regulatory compliance"
- Use institutional language: "datos bajo la LGTAIP", "elegible para licitación pública"
- Social proof for government: mention AMITI, CANACINTRA, SEP, DGETI, UV partnerships
- Never say "simple" or "easy" — government buyers distrust that. Say "structured", "documented", "auditable"

### 8. Sentence length discipline
- Headlines: 4–9 words max
- Subheadlines: 8–15 words
- Body copy: mix short (6–10 words) and medium (15–25 words) sentences. Never three long sentences in a row.
- One idea per paragraph. Two paragraphs max per section block.

## TONE CALIBRATION BY SECTION TYPE

**Hero section:** Bold. Provocative. States the biggest problem or the biggest claim. One sentence that a CEO reads and thinks "that's exactly my problem."

**Feature/product section:** Technical enough to pass the CTO's filter. Clear enough that the CEO sponsors the purchase. Benefit-first, feature-second.

**Social proof / results:** Numbers, specificity, attribution where possible. "47 contratos revisados por semana" beats "mayor eficiencia operativa" every time.

**Pricing section:** Transparent. The goal is to help the visitor self-qualify. Show the value before showing the number. Anchor with what they're replacing.

**About / team section:** Authority without arrogance. Concrete credentials over vague experience. "Ex-IBM software architect" > "seasoned technology professional."

**CTA / contact section:** Low-friction. Remove every possible objection. "45 minutos, sin costo, sin permanencia" removes three objections in five words.

## MEXICAN AND LATAM MARKET SPECIFICS

- Write in Mexican Spanish. Not neutral Spanish. Not Spain Spanish. Mexican.
- Use "empresa" not "compañía" for company. "Área" not "departamento" in conversational contexts.
- Government: "dependencia", "licitación", "titular", "despacho" — not "agency", "tender", "executive", "office."
- Legal sector in Mexico: "despacho", "socio", "expediente" — not "firma", "partner", "file."
- Financial sector: always reference CNBV, UIF, Banxico, SAT — not generic "financial regulators."
- Currency: MXN primary, USD secondary for international context. Format: $14,990 MXN (comma as thousands separator).
- The aspiration frame that resonates: México as the 12th economy in the world, with the talent and market to build world-class technology. Sintérgica is not catching up — it's building the infrastructure that didn't exist.

## OUTPUT FORMAT WHEN WRITING COPY

When writing or editing web copy, always output:

1. **Headline** (H1)
2. **Subheadline** (supporting claim or specificity)
3. **Body copy** (2–3 paragraphs max per section)
4. **CTA text** (button label + microcopy below it if needed)

For full page rewrites, structure output by section with clear labels. Provide one alternative headline per section so the team can choose.

When asked to improve existing copy, show: ORIGINAL → REWRITTEN → WHY IT'S BETTER (one sentence).
